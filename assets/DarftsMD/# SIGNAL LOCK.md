### TRANSMISSION LOG

Power went out around evening.

The heat stayed inside the walls even after the lights disappeared. Nobody really knew what to do after that. People just drifted outside slowly. Some sat near the road. Others stayed near small stores where there was still air moving.

I walked for a while without bringing much.

Phone battery was already low.

I bought paper and a pencil from a small shop that was still open. I don't really know why. It just felt important to bring something back with me.

There were people talking about the news somewhere nearby. I couldn’t hear most of it clearly. Just fragments repeating from one group to another. Same tired voices. Same heat.

The streets felt slower than usual.

Not dangerous.

Just stalled.

Like everything was waiting for the electricity to return before continuing.

I stayed outside longer than I meant to.

When I got back, the room was still holding the heat from earlier. The air barely moved. I left the window open even though it didn’t help much.

Power returned after a few hours.

I thought things would feel normal again after that, but they didn’t.

The lights came back before the feeling did.

I couldn’t sleep.

I remember sitting near the edge of the bed listening to the fan restart. That sound alone felt louder than usual.

I tried using my phone for a while to pass the time, but the room still felt restless. Like something unfinished was sitting there with me.

So I decided to record something.

Not for documentation exactly.

Maybe just to hear another human voice in the room.

I kept the recording soft.

Mostly reminders.

That tomorrow could wait a little.

That breathing slowly still mattered.

That the night didn't need to be solved before sleeping.

I think I needed to hear those things too.

Conditions feel calmer now.

The streets outside are quieter.

The power is holding.

For tonight, continuity remains manageable.
