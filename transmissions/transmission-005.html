The air moves differently here.

Not just stronger.

Less contained.

The wind keeps passing through before the atmosphere can fully settle into itself.

You can feel it constantly.

On skin. Across clothing. Through open spaces between buildings. Even inside conversations.

Sound behaves differently too.

Voices disappear faster once people move past you. The ocean carries over everything at a distance, but somehow never completely drowns anything out either.

There’s always movement underneath whatever you’re focusing on.

Waves. Wind. Traffic farther inland. Loose objects shifting slightly somewhere outside your field of view.

The environment never fully closes.

I noticed that the first evening near the shoreline.

The horizon looked wider than I expected.

Not visually.

Physically.

Like my sense of space kept extending farther than what I could comfortably process all at once.

At home, most things eventually return to walls.

Rooms. Hallways. Windows. Corners.

Even thoughts tend to settle into enclosed shapes after enough time.

Here, they don’t stay long enough for that.

The wind interrupts them before they fully stabilize.

At first I thought it was distracting.

But after staying outside longer, it started feeling more like dilution.

Not disappearance.

Just less accumulation.

Like the environment was carrying away small amounts of continuity before it could fully gather weight.

I walked near the shoreline for a while earlier without checking the time.

Eventually I realized I hadn’t reached for my phone in quite some time.

Not intentionally.

It just felt strangely smaller than the environment around it.

Like the device still belonged to enclosed spaces while everything here kept extending beyond the frame I was used to holding.

The nights feel different too.

Not quieter.

There’s actually more sound overall.

But the sound doesn’t collect in the same way.

At home, nighttime feels compressed.

Noise becomes trapped inside walls and ceilings and neighboring rooms.

Here, the darkness feels open instead of enclosed.

The sound disperses outward instead of settling nearby.

Even silence behaves differently between waves.

There are moments where the shoreline briefly empties itself enough that the wind becomes more noticeable than the water.

Then a few seconds later the balance changes again.

Nothing holds one shape for very long.

The ocean repeats itself constantly, but never exactly the same way twice.

Waves collapse differently. Wind direction shifts slightly. Conversations fade unevenly into distance.

The continuity here feels unstable too.

But not trapped.

Not compressed into walls or heat or closed curtains.

Just difficult to fully anchor.

Like the environment keeps extending beyond whatever frame I try to place around it.

For now…
