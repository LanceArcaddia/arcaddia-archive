TRANSMISSION LOG

The day didn’t really stay in one shape.

It kept breaking into smaller parts.

I would sleep, then wake up again without really feeling like I had finished sleeping.

It wasn’t sudden.

It was repetitive.

Like the rest of the day couldn’t fully decide when it should continue.

The room stayed warm even when I tried to rest.

Not intense all the time, just persistent.

Something that never fully left the concrete.

I kept waking up during the afternoon without knowing how much time had passed.

Each time, I would reach for water.

Ice Cold water.

Drink a bit.

Sometimes splash my face or neck or arms just to reset the feeling of being too warm.

Then I would try to sleep again.

It didn’t feel like starting over.

It felt like continuing something that never properly held its shape in the first place.

At some point, I stopped trying to measure the time in full stretches.

It became easier to just respond to what was happening in the moment.

Wake up. Cool down. Lie back down.

Repeat without planning it.

The curtains stayed closed for most of the afternoon.

It helped keep the heat from getting worse, but it also made the room feel like it wasn’t fully part of the outside anymore.

Just sealed in a softer version of the same air.

Later in the day, around evening, I got up again.

The light outside had changed enough that it didn’t feel like afternoon anymore, but the room still carried the leftover heat from the day.

I opened the windows.

Pulled the curtains fully aside.

Let the air move through properly for the first time that day.

It didn’t fix everything.

But it changed the way the room felt.

Like it could finally reset its temperature slightly.

After that, I stayed up for a while longer than I meant to.

Just sitting.

Not really doing anything.

Eventually, I tried to rest again.

But it still didn’t feel fully complete.

Like something in the cycle hadn’t closed properly yet.

For now…

continuity did not fully stabilize.
